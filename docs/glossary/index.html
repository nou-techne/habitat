Glossary

Key terms used throughout the Habitat system.

704(b) Compliance
The IRS regulation under Subchapter K that governs how partnership allocations must have "substantial economic effect." Habitat's patronage formulas are designed to satisfy 704(b) requirements, ensuring allocations reflect genuine economic activity. See Compliance.
Capital Account
A ledger tracking each member's economic interest in the cooperative. Capital accounts are adjusted by contributions, allocations of income or loss, and distributions. Maintaining accurate capital accounts is required for 704(b) compliance.
Composable Tools
Software components designed to be assembled in different configurations for different contexts, rather than delivered as a monolithic platform. Habitat is built as composable tools — each piece (identity, accounting, governance) works independently and together. See FAQ.
Contribution Lifecycle
The full arc of a contribution from initiation through verification to allocation. Every contribution passes through stages: proposal, execution, attestation, and recording as an economic event in the REA ledger.
Deficit Restoration Obligation (DRO)
A member's obligation to restore a negative capital account balance upon liquidation of the cooperative. DROs ensure that allocations of loss have real economic consequences, which is a requirement for substantial economic effect under 704(b). See Compliance.
Economic Habitat Matrix
The two-axis framework mapping organizations by governance orientation (concentrate vs. disperse) and systemic relationship (deplete vs. enrich). Habitat is designed for the mutualistic quadrant — dispersed governance, ecosystem enrichment. See Matrix.
ENS (Ethereum Name Service)
A decentralized naming system on Ethereum that maps human-readable names to addresses. Habitat uses ENS subdomains under habitat.eth as the identity layer for all agents. See Identity.
Event Sourcing
An architectural pattern where state changes are stored as a sequence of immutable events rather than overwriting current state. Habitat uses event sourcing to maintain a complete, auditable history of every economic action in the system.
Grant Pool (pool.habitat.eth)
The Ethereum address that holds funds designated for ecosystem grants. The grant pool distributes resources to projects and contributors aligned with Habitat's cooperative mission, governed by patronage-weighted decisions.
Limited Cooperative Association (LCA)
A legal entity under the Uniform Limited Cooperative Association Act that combines cooperative governance with the ability to accept both patron and investor members. RegenHub, LCA is Habitat's operating entity, filed under Colorado law. See FAQ.
Patronage
The measure of a member's economic participation in the cooperative. Surplus is allocated in proportion to patronage rather than capital investment, ensuring that those who contribute the most receive the most. See Patronage.
Patronage Formula
The mathematical formula that converts recorded contributions (service credits, capital, and other inputs) into patronage allocations. The formula must satisfy 704(b) substantial economic effect requirements. See Patronage.
Peer Production License
A CopyFarLeft license that permits free use by cooperatives, collectives, and non-profits while requiring for-profit enterprises under traditional corporate governance to obtain a commercial license. See License FAQ.
Qualified Income Offset (QIO)
A provision allowing members who receive unexpected allocations that create or increase a deficit in their capital account to be allocated income to offset that deficit. QIO is one of the safe harbor provisions under 704(b). See Compliance.
REA (Resource, Event, Agent)
An accounting ontology that models economic activity as events where agents exchange resources. Developed by William McCarthy, REA captures the full semantic context of exchanges rather than reducing them to debits and credits. Habitat uses REA as its foundational data model. See Matrix.
Service Credits
The unit of account for non-monetary contributions to the cooperative. Service credits track labor, expertise, and other non-capital inputs, making them visible in patronage calculations.
Seven Progressive Design Patterns
The foundational decomposition of all information systems into seven layered patterns: Identity, State, Relationship, Event, Flow, Constraint, and View. Each layer depends on those beneath it. See Thesis Patterns and FAQ.
Solar Audit
Habitat's annual review cycle aligned with solstices and equinoxes. Each quarter corresponds to a phase of the audit: contribution review, allocation, distribution, and planning. See Compliance.
Subchapter K
The section of the Internal Revenue Code governing taxation of partnerships and their partners. Cooperatives structured as LCAs operate under Subchapter K, which defines rules for allocations, distributions, and capital account maintenance. See Compliance.
Superfluid
An Ethereum protocol for programmable money streams — continuous, real-time token transfers. Habitat uses Superfluid for streaming patronage distributions and ongoing compensation flows rather than discrete periodic payments.
Watershed (watershed.habitat.eth)
The primary operational Ethereum address for Habitat. Named for the geographic metaphor of water flowing to where it is needed, the watershed contract manages the core treasury and coordination flows.
